Summary

Why Bonbids?

.......

This project was completed as a part of Beginex, a 12 week User Experience Design program that partners with non-profit organizations. For the cohort I was part of the client was Bonbids, an online fundraising platform where users participate in a timed auction that allows them to support causes they care about in a fun way, and win prizes for themselves in the process.

While Bonbids was already live, the client enlisted the Beginex cohort to delve into the design and suggest ways in which the platform could be improved. My group of 4 people was tasked with focusing on improving the onboarding process for new users.

OVERVIEW

The Challenge

What Problems am I Solving for?

.......

The goal of this project was to improve the user onboarding process. Though a seemingly small task, user onboarding is paramount to a product and, as defined by Chameleon, is "the system of actively guiding users to find new value in [the] product,"

User research showed us that the value of Bonbids lay in users understanding how the game worked, and feeling connected and engaged to their cause.

The Solution

Understand the Game & Feel Connected to the Cause

.......

Based on the insights gleaned from our user research, we focused on creating a user-centric and transparent onboarding process by:

  • More clearly explaining how the game works and creating a greater sense of connection to their cause.
  • Walking donors through onboarding using non-fundraiser terminology, providing them with more flexibility in payments, and transparency about how their donation would be used.
  • Creating a visually consistent and structurally simple onboarding process.

Competitive analysis

Learning From Existing Solutions

.......

After figuring out the challenges we were solving for, we jumped right into researching competitor sites so that we could understand where Bonbids fit into the market and learn from the strengths and weaknesses of competitor’s own onboarding process.

Flexible sign up options available to user. Helper text during sign-up process. 24/7 assistance offered.

Prompt to “sign-up” only occurs after clicking “sign-in.” Only fundraisers can create an account (not necessarily bad).

User-centric messaging and close connection to cause. Reminders for engagement and follow up.

Only can create account after you choose a project to fund.

Offers flexible options for payment (bids or credit card).

Users have to purchase tokens or put in cc info before playing.

We then researched what leading onboarding processes consisted of to determine how we might integrate the practices into the Bonbids onboarding process, focusing on the challenges we were seeking to solve:

Reverses funnel - begins with value before user registration.

Includes progress bars and playful tool tips.

Provides flexible sign up options.

Has simple and consistent displays.

Short 23 second video to help users quickly understand.

Follows a ‘Do, Show & Tell’ framework for effective understanding.

User research

Clarifying Users' Expectations & Frustrations

.......

Our user research consisted of interviewing five users, questioning their montivations for making online donations, and testing the usability of the current Bonbids onboarding process. All users were parents of middle school aged children who had made an online donation to their children's school within the last year.

Through our user research we identified 3 key themes that caused users to experience frustrations during the onboarding process:

  1. User-centricity & personalization
    • Users were confused by the language and fundraising terminology.
    • Users wanted flexibility and greater options in initial payment bundles.
  2. Simplicity & consistency
    • The onboarding steps and rationale for certain requirements were unclear.
    • Visual inconsistencies in the website design confused users.
  3. Engagement
    • Lack of reminders and clear next steps after onboarding hindered user participation.
    • Users wanted to feel a closer connection to the causes they were donating to.

User persona

Bringing a Bonbids User to Life

.......

With the information gained from our competitive analysis and user research, we put together a user persona, a hypothetical archetype of a real Bonbids user as defined by their motivations, goals, and frustrations. Building personas allowed us to summarize the patterns we noticed and give them life in the form of a fictional user.

ROB
45 year-old financial advisor with two children in middle school. Rob donates to several different organizations throughout the year that his children are involved in, i.e. their school to improve facilities and after-school programming.


Goals
  • Feel connected to the cause.
  • Know exactly where his money is going to.

"I only donate to causes I care about and where there is transparency about what my donation is being used for."


Frustrations
  • Doesn’t have a lot of free time to spend donating to cause.
  • Not technologically saavy.
